Required Before Any Work Begins
We verify ownership before touching your device or data — no exceptions.

South African law requires us to confirm you are the lawful owner or authorised user of any device or account before we perform access recovery or extract data from it. This protects you, protects us, and ensures this service cannot be misused. It takes under five minutes and is done in-store on arrival.

What you need to bring (two forms required):

  • Form 1 — Government-issued photo ID: South African ID card, passport, or driver's licence. Must match the name the device or account is registered to.
  • Form 2 — Proof of ownership (choose one):
    • Original proof of purchase (receipt, bank statement showing purchase, or retailer invoice)
    • IMEI registration on your name (bring the box, or log into your network provider account showing the device IMEI)
    • Access to the registered account on a separate device (e.g., sign into iCloud.com or Google in-store on another phone to show account ownership)
    • For MacBooks: system serial number matching your Apple ID registered devices, or MacBook listed in iCloud.com under your login
  • Signed job card declaration: You sign a written statement before work starts confirming you are the lawful owner. This is a standard part of our job card process for all access and data recovery work.

This policy is in place to comply with the Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020 (unlawful access to computer systems), the ECT Act 25 of 2002, and POPIA. Any person who provides false ownership information commits a criminal offence. MobiDokta reserves the right to refuse any job where ownership cannot be reasonably verified, without obligation to provide reasons.

Our Limits

What We Will Not Do

These boundaries protect you and protect us. A service that bypasses them puts both parties at legal risk under South African law — we refuse these jobs regardless of the reason given, without exception.

iCloud Activation Lock Bypass
iCloud Activation Lock is Apple's stolen device protection system. Bypassing it is not technically possible without Apple's involvement, and any service claiming to do so is either fraudulent or handling stolen goods. We do not offer this, and we cannot facilitate it.
Third-Party Account Access
We will not access, recover, or unlock any account, device, or data belonging to a person who is not present and has not provided written consent. "It's my spouse's phone" or "my employee's laptop" without documentation does not qualify.
Corporate MDM Bypass Without Authorisation
Removing an employer's MDM profile from a company device without that employer's written consent is a violation of the Cybercrimes Act and potentially the ECTA. We require written company authorisation on letterhead before touching MDM-enrolled devices.
Any Job Without Verified Ownership
If a client cannot provide government-issued ID plus proof of ownership, we will not start the job. We reserve the right to refuse without providing reasons, and we will not be pressured into exceptions regardless of the explanation offered.
Devices Flagged as Stolen
If a device returns as stolen on an IMEI check, or if we receive a legitimate police enquiry about a device in our possession, we will cooperate fully with law enforcement and will not return the device to the person who brought it in.
Google FRP or Samsung FRP Bypass
Factory Reset Protection is a theft deterrent. Bypassing it facilitates the use of stolen devices. We guide official account recovery only. Any client who cannot verify their Google or Samsung account during FRP recovery will be redirected to their carrier or the manufacturer.

Any person misrepresenting their ownership of a device to obtain access recovery services commits a criminal offence under the Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020 and the ECT Act 25 of 2002. MobiDokta maintains signed job cards and identity records on every access recovery job, which are available to law enforcement on lawful request.

Common Questions

What People Ask Us

Will data recovery erase my phone?
Data recovery from a dead or non-booting device does not touch your phone — we extract data directly from the storage chip without restoring or erasing anything. For access recovery (locked/disabled phone), a restore is required which erases the device, but your data is then restored from your iCloud or Google backup.
Can you recover data from a completely dead phone that won't turn on at all?
Sometimes — it depends on why the phone is dead and whether the NAND memory chip is physically intact. If the board fault is in the power circuit and hasn't damaged the memory, chip-level extraction is possible. We assess this for free. If data extraction is not feasible after assessment, you pay nothing.
I forgot my iCloud password and my iPhone is disabled — can you get me back in?
Yes, but you must be able to complete Apple's official account recovery. We restore the iPhone using DFU mode (this erases it), then guide you through Apple's iforgot.apple.com to recover your Apple ID. Without a valid Apple ID, setup cannot be completed — the device will remain in Activation Lock. We guide every step, but Apple's servers make the final call on account access.
My external hard drive is clicking and the computer doesn't recognise it. Can you fix it?
Power it off immediately — every additional spin causes more damage. A clicking HDD has a failed read head or motor fault. Data recovery is still possible if the platters are undamaged. Bring it in for a free assessment. Do not attempt to run recovery software on it yourself — this can overwrite recoverable data or cause further mechanical damage.
What do you do with my data after recovery?
Recovered data is transferred to a storage medium you provide or purchase from us, and then deleted from our working drives on handover. We do not retain, copy, or access client data beyond what is necessary to complete the recovery. This is a POPIA compliance requirement, and it's also just the right thing to do.
The phone was bought second-hand and I don't have a receipt. Can you still help?
Yes, if you can verify ownership through another route — such as logging into the registered Apple ID or Google account on a separate device in-store. If you are unable to demonstrate any connection to the registered account, we cannot perform access recovery. For data recovery (not access), standard device ownership verification still applies.
My employer gave me this work MacBook and I forgot the login. What do I need?
Corporate and MDM-managed devices require a letter from your employer on company letterhead, signed by an authorised IT manager or director, stating that MobiDokta is permitted to perform the access recovery. Your own ID is also required. Without both, we cannot start the job — we will not override a company's IT policy without their authorisation.
What is the "no fix, no charge" policy for data recovery?
Diagnosis is always free. Before any paid work begins, we tell you exactly what we found and what recovery is possible. If we cannot recover your data or restore your access, you pay nothing beyond the diagnosis fee (R0 — diagnosis is free). Payment is only collected on successful recovery or access restoration.
