    8 warning signs of a stolen or problematic phone

    Know these before you meet a seller. These are the exact red flags our engineers look for.

    Warning 01

    Price is too good to be true

    An iPhone 15 Pro Max selling for R6,000 when market price is R12,000+ is almost always problematic. Steep discounts are the oldest signal in the book.

    Warning 02

    Seller won't let you dial *#06#

    Every legitimate phone displays its IMEI when you dial *#06#. A seller who refuses this — or provides a different IMEI — has something to hide.

    Warning 03

    Can't sign into iCloud or Google

    If the device won't let a new account sign in, it may still be linked to the previous owner — or activation-locked. This can make the phone a permanent brick.

    Warning 04

    No original box or proof of purchase

    Legitimate sellers usually keep original packaging. Its absence doesn't prove theft, but combined with other signs it raises serious questions.

    Warning 05

    Seller won't meet in a public place

    Insistence on meeting in private locations, at night, or with urgency is a behavioural red flag — especially for high-value devices.

    Warning 06

    IMEI doesn't match the box

    The IMEI on the device (under Settings → General → About or *#06#) must match the box label exactly. A mismatch means the device was swapped.

    Warning 07

    Screen looks slightly different

    A replaced screen on a stolen iPhone often looks subtly different — warmer or cooler tone, slightly different brightness, or Touch ID/Face ID not working. These are signs of a rebuilt phone.

    Warning 08

    Battery health suspiciously high

    If a "used" iPhone shows 100% battery health, the battery was likely replaced recently — possibly to disguise the phone's actual age or usage history.
