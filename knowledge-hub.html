Industry Intelligence

Companies Behind Your Devices

Understanding who makes your device, its components, and the repair ecosystem around it.

Apple Inc.
Consumer Electronics · Cupertino, CA
Founded 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Apple designs the entire iPhone stack — A-series chips, iOS, and hardware — in-house. The vertical integration means tight software-hardware optimisation but also restricts third-party repairability. Apple's M-series silicon (M1–M4) is among the most power-efficient processor designs ever made. Apple holds over 27% of global smartphone market share.
A-series / M-series chips iOS · macOS · iPadOS TSMC 3nm fab 27% global smartphone share
Samsung Electronics
Consumer Electronics · Suwon, South Korea
The world's largest smartphone manufacturer by volume. Samsung is unique in manufacturing its own displays (AMOLED panels), chips (Exynos), and memory (NAND flash) — meaning it supplies components to Apple and its own devices simultaneously. Galaxy S and Z series flagships use OLED panels that rival Apple's display technology. The Galaxy A series dominates South African mid-range sales.
AMOLED / QLED displays Exynos chipsets Android / One UI 20% global smartphone share
Qualcomm
Semiconductor · San Diego, CA
Qualcomm's Snapdragon processors power most Android flagship phones — including Samsung Galaxy S series in some regions, all OnePlus, Xiaomi, and OPPO flagships. Snapdragon 8 Elite (2024) rivals Apple A18 in benchmark performance. Qualcomm also designs the modem chips used in iPhone (until Apple's own modem arrives). When your Android phone overheats, it's often the Snapdragon modem or the GPU throttling under load.
Snapdragon 8 Elite FastCharge technology 5G modem leader AI Processing units
iFixit
Repair Platform · San Luis Obispo, CA
Founded 2003 by Kyle Wiens. iFixit publishes free, crowd-sourced device repair guides under Creative Commons license. Their repairability scoring system (1–10) has become the industry standard for measuring how repairable a device is — directly influencing purchasing decisions globally. iFixit also sells quality repair tools and OEM-grade parts. They are the most influential right-to-repair advocacy organisation in the world.
Free repair manuals Repairability scoring Right-to-Repair advocacy Repair parts supplier
REWA Technology
Repair Training & Parts · Shenzhen, China
REWA Technology is the global benchmark for professional board-level phone repair training. Based in Shenzhen — the world's electronics manufacturing hub — REWA's engineers perform chip-level microsoldering repairs that most shops cannot attempt. Microsoldering training Board-level diagnostics Shenzhen parts network 4M+ YouTube subscribers
GSMA
Telecoms Standards Body · London, UK
The GSMA (Global System for Mobile Communications Association) is the body that administers the global IMEI database. Every legitimate phone manufacturer registers its models with GSMA — this is the root of IMEI authentication. The GSMA Device Registry is the central blacklist that networks in 160+ countries subscribe to. When MobiDokta's TrackForYou service blacklists a stolen IMEI, it is submitted to the GSMA registry.

The most common device questions — answered by MobiDokta's engineers.

Fast iPhone battery drain has four main causes: (1) Battery health below 80% — iOS throttles CPU speed below this threshold, paradoxically causing more drain. Check Settings → Battery → Battery Health. (2) Background App Refresh — disable for apps you don't need real-time updates from. (3) Screen brightness — the display is the biggest battery consumer on any phone. (4) A rogue app — check Settings → Battery → Last 10 Days and look for an app using excessive background activity. A battery replacement (R550, 45 minutes) resolves health-related drain instantly.
Act in this exact order:
1. Power it off immediately — short circuits happen when current flows through wet components.
2. Do NOT plug it in or put it on wireless charge.
3. Do NOT put it in rice — rice does not absorb moisture from inside a sealed device and the dust accelerates corrosion.
4. Do NOT shake or blow on it — this pushes water deeper into the board.
5. Dry the exterior and bring it to MobiDokta as quickly as possible. We use professional ultrasonic cleaning to remove mineral deposits before they corrode your logic board. Success rates drop significantly after 24 hours.
Go to Settings → Battery → Battery Health & Charging. Your Maximum Capacity percentage shows how much charge your battery holds compared to when it was new. Below 80% means iOS activates performance management — your CPU is intentionally throttled to prevent unexpected shutdowns. You will notice slower app launches, reduced gaming performance, and more drain. A replacement at MobiDokta restores full capacity and lifts the throttle completely.
OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode): Each pixel produces its own light. Black pixels are completely off — giving true blacks and infinite contrast. More power-efficient on dark content. Thinner displays. Used in all iPhones from X onwards and all Samsung Galaxy S/Z flagships.

LCD (Liquid Crystal Display): A single backlight behind a liquid crystal filter. Blacks are actually dark grey. Better brightness uniformity and no risk of burn-in. More affordable to replace.

At MobiDokta we stock Hard OLED, Soft OLED and Incell LCD panels for most models and explain the differences in colour, brightness and price before you decide.

Random restarts have four main causes: (1) Failing battery — most common cause on phones 2+ years old. A battery with poor cell integrity drops voltage under load, triggering an emergency shutdown. (2) Overheating — the thermal management system shuts down the phone to prevent chip damage. (3) Software crash loop — a corrupted app or iOS update. Try a factory reset first. (4) Power management IC fault — a board-level component failure requiring microsoldering. Start with a battery replacement — it resolves 70% of random restart cases.
Microsoldering is component-level board repair — soldering individual chips and connections as small as 0.2mm directly onto a phone's logic board using a microscope and precision equipment. It is used for: no-power faults, charging IC failure, touchscreen IC failure, WiFi/Bluetooth chip replacement, Face ID flex repair, and data recovery from physically damaged boards. Not every repair shop offers this service — it requires a hot air rework station, digital microscope, solder flux, BGA reballing stencils, and years of training. MobiDokta performs board-level diagnostics and microsoldering in-house.

The TAC (Type Allocation Code) is the first 8 digits of an IMEI number. It uniquely identifies the device manufacturer and model. When buying a second-hand phone, the TAC should match the device being sold — an iPhone 15 Pro has a different TAC range than an iPhone 14, for example. A mismatched TAC means the IMEI may have been swapped, which is a criminal offence. MobiDokta's CheckForYou service decodes and verifies the TAC as part of every IMEI check.
